Mechanism of Action

Osteovance® (terifosenib disodium)
 

Dear {{recipient.name}},

I would like to walk you through how Osteovance® works at the molecular level to promote bone formation in patients with osteoporosis.
1

Selective RANK Ligand Inhibition

Osteovance binds with high affinity to RANKL, preventing its interaction with the RANK receptor on osteoclast precursors. This interrupts the signaling cascade responsible for osteoclast differentiation and activation.
 
2

Osteoclast Activity Reduction

With reduced RANKL signaling, osteoclast-mediated bone resorption is significantly decreased. Bone turnover markers (CTX, NTX) show measurable reduction within 2 weeks of treatment initiation.
 
3

Wnt Pathway Activation

Simultaneously, the terifosenib moiety activates canonical Wnt signaling in osteoblasts through sclerostin inhibition. This promotes osteoblast differentiation and new bone matrix deposition.
 
4

Net Bone Formation Increase

The dual mechanism results in net positive bone balance: reduced resorption coupled with increased formation. BMD increases of 8.4% at the lumbar spine were observed at 12 months in the FOUNDATION trial.
