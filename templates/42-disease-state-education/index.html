Disease Overview
Rheumatoid arthritis is a chronic, systemic autoimmune disorder primarily affecting synovial joints. If left untreated, RA leads to progressive joint destruction, disability, and increased cardiovascular mortality. Early diagnosis and aggressive treatment are critical to achieving remission or low disease activity.

Current Treatment Landscape
csDMARDs - Methotrexate remains first-line; sulfasalazine and hydroxychloroquine as alternatives
bDMARDs - TNF inhibitors, IL-6 inhibitors, T-cell co-stimulation modulators, B-cell depleting agents
tsDMARDs - JAK inhibitors offering oral alternative to biologic therapies

Key Unmet Needs

Despite advances, approximately 30-40% of RA patients fail to achieve adequate response with current therapies. Challenges include treatment resistance, safety concerns with long-term immunosuppression, and the need for personalized treatment approaches based on molecular phenotyping.
